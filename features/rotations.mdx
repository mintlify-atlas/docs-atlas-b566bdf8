Rotations is a music player built around a unique concept: instead of endlessly scrolling through your library, you create focused **rotations** of music that evolve as you listen.

## How rotations work

When you add music to Rotations, every track automatically becomes part of your **current rotation**. As you listen, the system builds connections between tracks and playlists, creating a personalized music graph.

<Info>
Rotations is import-only. You add music once, and it's stored forever in your browser's local database.
</Info>

### Track associations

Every track in your library maintains connections to other tracks across different playlists. These connections are called **next tracks** and determine what you can jump to after the current song finishes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Import your music">
    Add playlists from Bandcamp, Spotify, YouTube, or SoundCloud. Each playlist becomes part of your library.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Associations are generated">
    The system automatically creates cross-playlist connections. Each track gets 2 random next tracks from other playlists.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Listen and explore">
    As you play music, you'll see side tracks that let you jump between playlists seamlessly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Side tracks

While listening to any track, you'll see **side tracks** positioned on the left and right of the album artwork. These are your next track options from different playlists.


This creates a non-linear listening experience where you can branch between genres, artists, and albums based on your mood.

<Note>
Your rotation data never leaves your device. Everything is stored client-side in localStorage and IndexedDB.
</Note>

## Player state persistence

Rotations automatically saves your listening position every 5 seconds, so you can close the app and resume exactly where you left off.

```typescript
// From site/src/hooks/PlayerContext.tsx:261-275
const savePlayerState = useCallback(() => {
    const trackId = tracks[currentTrackIndex]?.id
    if (trackId) {
        db.setPlayerState({ 
            activeTrack: trackId, 
            trackTimestamp: currentTimeMs 
        })
    }
}, [tracks, currentTrackIndex, currentTimeMs])

useEffect(() => {
    if (!currentTrack) return
    const interval = setInterval(savePlayerState, 5000)
    savePlayerState()
    return () => clearInterval(interval)
}, [currentTrackIndex, savePlayerState, currentTrack])
```
